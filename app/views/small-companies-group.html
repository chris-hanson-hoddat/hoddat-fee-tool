Groups whose members are always considered large

If your organisation is part of a group you may automatically be considered a large sponsor. This depends on the other members of your group.

All the members of a group will be considered large sponsors if any/a> member is:

  • a traded company
  • a body corporate (other than a company) whose shares are admitted to trading on a regulated market in an EEA State
  • a person (other than a small company) who has permission under Part 4 of the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (c. 8) to carry on a regulated activity
  • an e-money issuer
  • a authorised insurance company, a banking company, an e-money issuer, a MiFID investment firm or a UCITS management company
  • a person who carries on insurance market activity

At any time in your last full financial year, has your organisation been part of a group that includes any of these?
